# How-to-solder Soldering for beginers

What is Soldering?

Soldering is a joining process used to join different types of metals together by melting solder. Solder is a metal alloy usually made of tin and lead which is melted using a hot iron. The iron is heated to temperatures above 600 degrees fahrenheit which then cools to create a strong electrical bond. It's typical to solder when joining electrical wires and for making electrical joints when repairing or assembling electrical appliances and electronic equipment.

Materials needed for Soldering

1. Soldering iron - Iron is a great heat source and maintains a very consistent tempature. 2. Solder- Use thick solders for larger materials and thinner solders for more detailed work. 3. Soldering iron tips - Mainly choosing sizes for the tips. You want tip just a little bit smaller before than what you are soldering for control purposes. 4. Soldering iron holder and cleaning sponge - Gives you a nice place to set your soldering iron down in between sessions. 5. Tools to work with wires - Using tools such as wire cutters, a wire stripper, and needle nose pliers if working with tons of wire. 6. Clips to hold your workc- use alligator clips, clamps, or even some tape to hold things in place if you need to. 7. Exhaust fan - To clear out any fumes while soldering. 8. Safety goggles - For Safety purposes such as bits and pieces flying everywhere.
